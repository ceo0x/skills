# Comprehensive Audit Framework

Use this to evaluate any existing onboarding. Score each item:
- **0** — Missing
- **1** — Partial / weak
- **2** — Strong

Total possible: 70 (or 62 if the product has no mobile app — skip the Mobile section).

## Speed & Friction (10 pts)

- [ ] TTFV under 10 minutes for the primary use case
- [ ] Initial wizard is 7 steps or fewer
- [ ] No unnecessary form fields before value delivery
- [ ] OAuth/SSO available (not just email/password)
- [ ] Progress persists across sessions

## Activation & Value (10 pts)

- [ ] Aha Moment clearly defined and measurable
- [ ] Flow optimized to reach Aha Moment as fast as possible
- [ ] First win happens within the onboarding flow (not after)
- [ ] Empty states guide to first action
- [ ] Templates or demo data available for complex products

## Personalization (6 pts)

- [ ] At least 1 segmentation dimension used
- [ ] Flow adapts based on user segment
- [ ] Segmentation captured in 1 question or fewer

## Engagement & Psychology (10 pts)

- [ ] Progress indicator visible throughout
- [ ] Checklist with actionable items and endowed progress
- [ ] Micro-celebrations at key milestones
- [ ] Copy leads with benefits, includes time estimates
- [ ] Social proof present at commitment moments

## Multi-Channel (6 pts)

- [ ] Behavior-triggered email sequence in place
- [ ] Re-engagement email for inactive users (day 3-7)
- [ ] Human touchpoint available for high-value accounts

## Measurement (10 pts)

- [ ] All critical analytics events instrumented
- [ ] Funnel visualization with step-by-step drop-off
- [ ] TTFV tracked and segmented
- [ ] A/B tests running on at least one element
- [ ] Weekly review of onboarding metrics

## Accessibility & Polish (10 pts)

- [ ] Keyboard navigable
- [ ] Screen reader compatible
- [ ] Mobile responsive
- [ ] Loading/error states handled gracefully
- [ ] Reduced motion respected

## Mobile-Specific (8 pts — score only if product has a mobile app)

- [ ] Deferred sign-in or guest mode available
- [ ] Permission prompts come after value, with pre-prompt context
- [ ] Push opt-in timed to moment of contextual need
- [ ] Deep links from marketing route to campaign-specific flows

## Scoring Interpretation

| Score | Interpretation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0-20 | Critical rebuild needed | Stop optimizing individual parts. Redesign from the pattern selection down. |
| 21-35 | Targeted improvements | Fix the weakest 1-2 categories. Expect meaningful activation lift. |
| 36-50 | Optimization phase | You have a working flow. Move to A/B testing specific elements. |
| 51-60 | Strong | Refine with Tier 2-3 experiments. Focus on expansion/advocacy. |
| 61+ | World-class | Maintain, stay measured, watch for degradation. |

## How to Use

1. Score each item honestly. "Partial" is 1 — don't round up.
2. Identify the lowest-scoring category. That's your next investment.
3. Inside that category, pick the lowest-scoring items first.
4. Re-score after each sprint to see movement.

A skilled reviewer can complete this audit in 30 minutes. Get a second scorer to cross-check — scoring drift is real, especially on the "partial" middle ground.

## Using the Audit on Your Own Design

When you've just designed or built an onboarding flow, run this audit on your own output before declaring done. If you scored below 26, revise before handing off. A low-scoring design is a future rebuild you're handing the user.
