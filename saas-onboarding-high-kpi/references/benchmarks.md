# Onboarding Benchmarks

Use these when citing target metrics or setting goals. Sources and last-reviewed date at the bottom — refresh annually.

## Cross-Industry SaaS Benchmarks

| Metric | Bottom Quartile | Average SaaS | Top Quartile | World-Class | Recommended Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Time-to-First-Value (TTFV) | > 48 hours | 7-36 hours | 5-15 min | < 3 min | < 10 min |
| Activation Rate | < 20% | 25-40% | 55-70% | > 75% | > 60% |
| Onboarding Completion Rate | < 40% | 50-70% | 85%+ | > 92% | > 85% |
| Trial-to-Paid Conversion | < 5% | 8-25% | 30-50% | > 55% | > 35% |
| 7-Day Retention | < 30% | 40-50% | 65%+ | > 80% | > 65% |
| 30-Day Retention | < 15% | 20-35% | 45%+ | > 60% | > 45% |
| Setup Completion (Day 1) | < 30% | 40-55% | 70%+ | > 85% | > 70% |
| Time to Complete Onboarding | > 30 min | 10-25 min | 3-8 min | < 2 min | < 8 min |

## Caveats When Citing

- These are medians across public data. Your product's targets depend on your category, ACV, and buyer.
- Benchmarks drift. Re-check the source material annually.
- Compare to your own past performance as the primary signal. External benchmarks set direction; internal baselines set the line.
- Top-quartile numbers come from products with specific conditions (strong PMF, category leadership). Don't assume you can hit them without those conditions.
- Be skeptical of quoted numbers without sources. "Industry average is X" without citation is usually made up.
